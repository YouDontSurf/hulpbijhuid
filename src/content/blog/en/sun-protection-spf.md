## Why is Sun Protection Important?

UV radiation is the leading cause of skin aging and a major risk factor for skin cancer. Daily sun protection is essential.

## SPF Explained

SPF (Sun Protection Factor) indicates how well a sunscreen filters UVB radiation:

- **SPF 15:** 93% UVB filtering
- **SPF 30:** 97% UVB filtering
- **SPF 50:** 98% UVB filtering

## UVA vs. UVB

- **UVB:** Causes sunburn, plays a role in skin cancer
- **UVA:** Penetrates deeper, causes aging and wrinkles
- **Choose broad spectrum:** Protects against both

## How Much and How Often

Use about 2 milligrams per square centimeter of skin (a full teaspoon for the face, a shot glass for the body). Reapply every 2 hours and after swimming or sweating.

## Tips

- Use SPF 30 or higher, even on cloudy days
- Apply sunscreen 15-30 minutes before exposure
- Don't forget ears, neck, back of hands, and lips
- Use year-round, even in winter
