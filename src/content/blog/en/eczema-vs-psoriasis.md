## Eczema vs. Psoriasis: What's the Difference?

Eczema and psoriasis look similar but are different conditions requiring different treatments.

## Appearance

**Eczema:** Red, inflamed skin that may ooze and crust. The skin often looks raw.

**Psoriasis:** Thick, silvery scales on a red base. Patches are sharply defined.

## Itch

**Eczema:** Very intense itching, often worse at night. Scratching provides temporary relief.

**Psoriasis:** Mild to moderate itching. Often more of a burning or painful sensation.

## Location

**Eczema:** Bending sides of elbows and knees, neck, face, hands.

**Psoriasis:** Extending sides of elbows and knees, lower back, scalp, nails.

## Treatment

Both are treated with corticosteroids, but psoriasis responds well to phototherapy and biologics, while eczema benefits more from intensive hydration.

Consult a dermatologist for proper diagnosis and treatment.
